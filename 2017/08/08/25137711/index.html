Space station astronauts discover Earth's eerie blue patches"! It's the treasure of the peasants

August 8, 2017 本文总阅读量次

Since the construction of the international space station, station mission astronauts in the international space in the outer space for a variety of scientific experiments, but also to the international space station astronauts provide a unique perspective to observe the earth. They were able to take pictures of the spectacular spectacle of the earth with their cameras.


Recently, astronauts at the International Space Station surprised the camera with a strange blue patch in a tan field”!


This patch looks very strange in space. The blue color is quite different from what we see in the sea and the lake, where white stripes are mixed.
In fact, this “blue and white stripe patch”, which looks out of place, is filmed in Utah, the United States, and its true face is a solar energy evaporating pool in Utah!


It looks deserted here. Land is bare, Tan, but also rich in minerals, so people build evaporation ponds here to produce related products. The evaporation pool is 400 acres, with twenty-seven colorful ponds. The main thing that this evaporation pool produces is sylvite, which is one of the common fertilizers in agricultural production.
Potassium is a necessary nutrient for the growth of crops, so the demand is very large, but not every place can produce potassium salt, and here is one of them. The Qaidam Basin in the interior of China is also one of the famous potash producing areas.


The strange appearance of the blue is that the evaporation pool is added to the dye, and the deep blue dye is added to the saturated potassium brine to accelerate the absorption of heat, thus gradually crystallizing the potash. The color of each pond represents a different state of evaporation. The light color indicates that a large amount of salt has been precipitated, and that the tan pond is almost dry, and the potash crystals (final products) remain in the pond and are ready to collect.


In the scenery under the shadow of Utah shows CIC desert landscape, compared with the Colorado Valley, the average elevation of Colorado plateau 1600 meters above mean sea level (5200 feet), with an average altitude of 1200 meters (3900 feet). The Colorado River washes out deep canyons, with darker green vegetation in the river banks. A close observation of the northwest and the highway allows you to enjoy the magnificent landscape of the Colorado River Valley by way of the highway.
