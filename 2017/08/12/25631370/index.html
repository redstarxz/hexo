Let’s look back at Li Di, deputy chief engineer of the FAST project. Before joining the FAST project, Li Di made his mark in the field of radio astronomy in the United states.
In May 2002, Li Di graduated from the Cornell University in the United States and became an astronomer and researcher in the United states.
In 2010, Li Di made outstanding contributions to the world’s largest space telescope, the Herschel telescope, and won the NASA team award as the only Chinese scientist in the team’s award.
In 2012, Li was determined to return to China and take part in the construction of the FAST line. “It’s lucky for me to be able to meet world-class projects like FAST. It’s also my science’s interest. There’s no reason not to come back.”.”
