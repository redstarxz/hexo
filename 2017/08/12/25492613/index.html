One of China's richest provinces: the Qing Dynasty was divided into two provinces, and now the gap between the two provinces is great

August 11, 2017 本文总阅读量次


In the splendid history of China’s five thousand li, its territory is constantly changing. Whenever a dynasty appears, great changes will take place in every administrative region of the territory of china. Today, the administrative divisions of China are closely related to the Qing dynasty. Today I would like to introduce the story of the richest province in the Qing Dynasty, the province of jiangnan.
Jiangnan province was originally the area around Nanjing in Ming Dynasty, probably around Jiangsu, Anhui and Shanghai. That is to say, at that time, the province of Jiangnan was the southern Zhili Province in the early Ming dynasty”. However, whether it was the Ming Dynasty “Southern Zhili Province” or the later Qing Dynasty “Jiangnan province”, it was the richest province in the country at that time.


After the Manchus, the total tax only Jiangnan province this provinces Shanghai accounted for 1/3 of the total national tax revenue. In addition to Jiangnan province rich, there is also talented people. Statistics show that the Jiangnan province each examination results, the list number accounted for more than half of the country, “the world of excellence, as much as half of South of the yangtze!”
So the question is, why are such important provinces divided into two? This should begin with the political environment of the Qing dynasty. After the Manchus, the country did not achieve complete reunification, constantly throughout the uprising. For example, the southeast of Nanming and southwest of the rebels, with the royal family is not monolithic, internal contradictions.


At the same time, the province was an important source of taxes and talents for the Qing Dynasty, so stability was the top priority here. Eighteen years of emperor Shunzhi, this will Jiangnan province that is in accordance with the division of One divides into two., north-south direction, namely: “Jiangnan right department”, and “left Suzhou Jiangnan Secretary Buzheng Shi Jiangning”.
When Kangxi was six years, Kangxi again made the decision, “synthesis of Jiangning” and “Suzhou” two house song words, “Jiangnan right department” to “Jiangsu Department”, “Jiangsu province”; the synthesis of “Anqing” and “Huizhou” two house song words, “Jiangnan the Secretary Buzheng left” as the “Anhui Department”, “construction of Anhui province”.


After being divided into two provinces, Anhui and Jiangsu, the Qing Dynasty was still the two richest place in the Qing Dynasty. Until the Qing Dynasty came to an end, the economic levels of the two provinces were in the middle and upper reaches of the country. Careful readers may find a problem, why not Qing Jiangnan province was One divides into two.?
In fact, this is the result of the comprehensive consideration of the Qing government. If there was division of Jiangnan province is retained, the important part of Jiangnan Province, which is not conducive to the elimination of the threat of Jiangnan Province, the final weigh repeatedly chose vertical division. Since the division of Jiangnan Province, the gap between the two provinces has gradually shown up in the modern times.


In 2016, the province’s urban GDP total value and growth rate, Jiangsu province ranks second, while Anhui is ranked thirteenth, the gap between the two is still very large. However, in recent years, Anhui’s economic development is also very fast, for example, in 2016, Anhui, Hefei provincial capital housing prices rose more alarming.
